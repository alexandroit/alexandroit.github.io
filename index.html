About

Revive what the market still depends on.

The project exists to keep important libraries useful after original maintenance slows down, so businesses do not have to choose between outdated dependencies and expensive rewrites.

Build

Create the libraries teams still need.

ReviveJS is not limited to maintenance. It also creates modern packages where practical product teams still face gaps in frontend infrastructure.

Port

Translate proven ideas across ecosystems.

When a useful library exists only in React, the same value can be adapted for Angular, Vue, or Vanilla JS so teams are not locked into a single framework culture.

Why it matters

Open-source continuity has real business value.

For companies running production frontend systems, abandoned libraries create cost, delay, compatibility pressure, and avoidable operational risk. ReviveJS turns that risk into a modernization path by preserving familiar developer experience while aligning packages with current frameworks and tooling.

Continuity

Preserve working product investments.

Teams can continue using proven patterns instead of replacing entire UI workflows because a package stopped receiving updates.

Compatibility

Support current framework releases.

Maintaining compatibility with modern Angular, React, and Vue versions helps businesses upgrade platforms with less friction.

Portability

Spread value beyond one community.

Useful ideas should not stay trapped in a single ecosystem. Cross-framework adaptation expands access to proven solutions.

Efficiency

Reduce the cost of forced rewrites.

Revived packages lower migration pressure and protect engineering time that can stay focused on product outcomes.

National-scale relevance Modernized libraries help companies upgrade faster, keep legacy applications usable, and avoid expensive dependency dead ends.

That combination matters to organizations maintaining large internal systems, customer-facing products, and long-lived enterprise platforms. The public benefit comes from stronger digital continuity, reduced technical debt pressure, and broader access to modernized tooling.

Operating model
1. Revive

Take unmaintained packages and bring them up to date with current framework majors, packaging standards, and documentation.

2. Extend

Fill market gaps with practical libraries that engineering teams can adopt quickly.

3. Adapt

Move proven concepts across Angular, React, Vue, and Vanilla JS whenever that creates real utility for teams.
